## About Company
Tibber Deutschland GmbH is a German electricity provider that offers smart energy tariffs to consumers. They provide digital services for managing electricity consumption.

## Incident and Exposure
On November 10, 2024, Tibber Deutschland GmbH experienced a data breach affecting approximately 50,000 customer records. The incident resulted in the exposure of personal information, including names, email addresses, geographic locations (specifically city and postcode), and the total amount spent on purchases. While the exact method of the breach has not been publicly detailed beyond its confirmation, the compromised data points indicate an unauthorized access to Tibber's customer database.

The exposed data carries several implications for affected individuals. Email addresses are frequently used as identifiers for online accounts, making them valuable for phishing attempts. Names and geographic locations, when combined, can be used to personalize these phishing emails, making them appear more legitimate and increasing the likelihood of a victim clicking on malicious links or divulging further sensitive information. The inclusion of purchase totals, while not inherently sensitive, could potentially be used by attackers to infer financial habits or target individuals based on their spending patterns.

## Next Steps
If you are a Tibber customer, you should immediately change your password for your Tibber account and any other online accounts where you have used the same or a similar password. Reusing passwords across multiple services significantly increases your risk if one service is compromised. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on your Tibber account and any other critical online services where it is available. MFA adds an extra layer of security, typically requiring a code from your phone in addition to your password, making it much harder for unauthorized parties to access your accounts even if they have your password.

Be highly vigilant for phishing attempts. Attackers often use data from breaches to craft convincing emails or messages. Scrutinize any communication claiming to be from Tibber or other services, especially those asking for personal information, login credentials, or directing you to click on links. Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders. Monitor your financial statements and credit reports for any unusual activity. While financial account numbers were not reported as exposed in this breach, the combination of personal data could be used in identity theft attempts. Consider placing a fraud alert on your credit file with major credit bureaus if you are concerned about potential misuse of your exposed information.
