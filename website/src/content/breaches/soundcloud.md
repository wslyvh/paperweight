## About Company
SoundCloud is an online audio distribution platform and music sharing website based in Berlin, Germany, that allows its users to upload, promote, and share audio.

## Incident and Exposure
On December 15, 2025, SoundCloud Limited announced a data breach affecting approximately 29.8 million user records. The company detected unauthorized activity on its platform, which led to an attacker gaining access to and mapping publicly available SoundCloud profile data to user email addresses.

The exposed data includes avatars, email addresses, geographic locations (in some cases, the user's country), names, profile statistics (such as follower and following counts), and usernames. While no passwords or financial information were compromised, the combination of email addresses with other personal identifiers increases the risk of targeted phishing attacks and spam. Attackers could use this information to craft convincing fraudulent emails or messages, attempting to gain access to other online accounts or sensitive data.

Following the breach, the attackers attempted to extort SoundCloud. When their demands were not met, the compromised data was publicly released the following month. This public release means the exposed information is now widely accessible, increasing the long-term risk of misuse.

## Next Steps
Given the exposure of your email address and other personal details, it is crucial to remain vigilant against phishing attempts. Be suspicious of any unsolicited emails or messages that appear to be from SoundCloud or other services, especially those asking for login credentials, personal information, or directing you to unfamiliar websites. Always verify the sender and the legitimacy of links before clicking.

While your SoundCloud password was not compromised in this incident, it is a good practice to review the security of your other online accounts. If you used the same or a similar password for your SoundCloud account as you do for other services, change those passwords immediately. Consider enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) on all your important online accounts where available, including SoundCloud, to add an extra layer of security.

Monitor your email inbox for any unusual activity or an increase in spam. Be cautious of any communications that seem too good to be true or create a sense of urgency. Regularly review the privacy settings on your SoundCloud profile and other social media accounts to control what information is publicly visible.
