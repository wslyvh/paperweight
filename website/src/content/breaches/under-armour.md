## About Under Armour
Under Armour, Inc. is an American company that designs, develops, markets, and sells athletic apparel, footwear, and accessories.

## Incident and Exposure
In November 2025, the Everest ransomware group claimed responsibility for a cyberattack on Under Armour. The group alleged they had exfiltrated 343GB of data and attempted to extort a ransom from the company. Following non-payment, customer data from this incident was subsequently published publicly on a popular hacking forum in January 2026.

This breach exposed 72.7 million records. The compromised data includes email addresses, names, dates of birth, genders, geographic locations, and purchase information. The combination of email addresses with other personal identifiers and purchase history increases the risk of targeted phishing attacks and identity theft.

The exposure of email addresses, names, and geographic locations can be used for social engineering attempts. Dates of birth and genders, when combined with other data, can make it easier for malicious actors to impersonate individuals or gain access to other accounts. Purchase information could be used to craft highly convincing phishing scams related to past transactions.

## Next Steps
You should immediately change the password for your Under Armour account if you have one. Choose a strong, unique password that you do not use for any other online service. If you reuse passwords, change them on any other accounts where you used the same password as your Under Armour account. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on your Under Armour account and any other online services that offer it.

Remain vigilant for suspicious emails, texts, or phone calls. Be cautious of any communication claiming to be from Under Armour or other companies that asks for personal information, login credentials, or financial details. Verify the legitimacy of such requests directly with the company through official channels, not by responding to the suspicious communication.

Monitor your financial accounts and credit reports for any unauthorized activity. While financial data was not directly reported as exposed in this breach, the combination of personal information can facilitate identity theft. Consider placing a fraud alert or security freeze on your credit reports with the three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion) to prevent new accounts from being opened in your name.
