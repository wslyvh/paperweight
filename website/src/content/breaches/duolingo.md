## About Company
Duolingo, Inc. is a language-learning platform that offers courses in various languages through its website and mobile app.

## Incident and Exposure
On January 24, 2023, data scraped from Duolingo's platform was initially offered for sale. This data contained 2.7 million user records. The information was later widely distributed on a hacking forum in August 2023. The breach occurred by exploiting a vulnerable API, allowing unauthorized parties to collect user data.

The exposed data includes email addresses, names, spoken languages, and usernames. While some of this information, such as usernames and spoken languages, may be publicly visible on the Duolingo platform, the exposure of private email addresses linked to these public profiles creates a significant privacy risk. This combination of public and private data can be used for targeted phishing attempts or other social engineering attacks.

## Next Steps
You should immediately change your Duolingo password to a strong, unique password that you do not use for any other online accounts. If you have reused your Duolingo password on other websites or services, change those passwords as well. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your Duolingo account and any other accounts where it is available, as this adds an extra layer of security beyond just a password.

Be highly vigilant for phishing emails or messages that appear to be from Duolingo or other services. Attackers may use the exposed email addresses and usernames to craft convincing scams. Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders. Monitor your email inbox for any unusual activity, such as password reset requests you did not initiate. Consider setting up a dedicated email address for sensitive accounts to minimize exposure in future breaches.
