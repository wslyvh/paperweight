## About Company
Bouygues Telecom S.A. is a major French telecommunications provider offering mobile, landline, internet, and television services.

## Incident and Exposure
On August 4, 2025, Bouygues Telecom experienced a cyberattack that led to a data breach. This incident exposed approximately 5.7 million unique customer records. The compromised data includes sensitive personal information such as your full name, physical address, phone number, date of birth, email address, and bank account number (IBAN).

The exposure of this data carries significant risks. Your name, address, and phone number can be used for targeted phishing attempts or social engineering scams. Your date of birth, combined with other exposed data, could aid in identity theft. Most critically, the exposure of your bank account number (IBAN) increases the risk of unauthorized transactions or financial fraud. While Bouygues Telecom stated that affected customers have been notified, the broad scope of exposed data means vigilance is necessary.

## Next Steps
Given the exposure of your personal and financial information, several immediate actions are recommended. First, change the password for your Bouygues Telecom account and any other online accounts where you may have used the same or a similar password. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts that offer it, especially for banking, email, and social media, to add an extra layer of security.

Second, closely monitor your bank statements and credit reports for any suspicious activity. Report any unauthorized transactions or unfamiliar accounts to your bank and the relevant credit bureaus immediately. Be highly cautious of unsolicited communications (emails, calls, or texts) claiming to be from Bouygues Telecom or your bank, as these could be phishing attempts designed to trick you into revealing more information. Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders.

Finally, be aware that your exposed information could be used for identity theft. Consider placing a fraud alert or credit freeze with credit reporting agencies to prevent new accounts from being opened in your name without your authorization. Remain vigilant for any signs of misuse of your personal data, and report any concerns to the appropriate authorities.
