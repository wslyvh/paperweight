## About Odido
Odido is a Dutch telecommunications provider offering mobile, internet, and television services to consumers and businesses.

## Incident and Exposure
On February 12, 2026, Odido experienced a data breach that led to an extortion attempt. Following this, data affecting 6.1 million records was released publicly in four stages over several days. The exposed information includes a wide range of personal identifiers and sensitive data.

Specifically, the breach compromised names, physical addresses, and phone numbers. It also exposed financial information in the form of bank account numbers. Further personal details such as dates of birth and genders were included. Highly sensitive government-issued identification numbers, including passport numbers, driver's license numbers, and European national ID numbers, were also compromised. Additionally, customer service comments were exposed, which could contain details about user interactions and service issues.

The exposure of such a comprehensive set of personal and financial data carries significant risks. Individuals are vulnerable to identity theft, financial fraud, and targeted phishing attacks. The combination of names, addresses, phone numbers, and bank account numbers can be used to impersonate individuals for financial gain. The inclusion of government-issued ID numbers further escalates the risk of sophisticated identity fraud.

## Next Steps
If your data was part of the Odido breach, you should immediately take steps to protect yourself. Change passwords for your Odido account and any other online accounts where you may have used the same or similar credentials. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts that support it, especially for banking, email, and social media, to add an extra layer of security.

Monitor your financial accounts closely for any suspicious activity. Review bank statements and credit card statements regularly for unauthorized transactions. Consider placing a fraud alert or credit freeze with credit bureaus to prevent new accounts from being opened in your name. Be highly suspicious of unsolicited communications, particularly emails, texts, or calls claiming to be from Odido or other organizations asking for personal information, as these could be phishing attempts.

Given the exposure of government-issued ID numbers, be vigilant for any official-looking documents or communications that seem out of place. If you suspect your identity has been stolen, report it to the relevant authorities and your bank. Refer to Odido's official disclosure and FAQ for additional guidance specific to this incident.
